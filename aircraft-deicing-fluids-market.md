<p><p>Aircraft deicing fluids are categorized into Type I, Type II, and Type IV based on their composition and performance. Type I is a thin, heated fluid used for quick ice removal, offering minimal holdover time. Type II is thicker, unheated, and provides longer protection against ice formation, often used in colder conditions. Type IV is the most viscous, offering the longest holdover time, and is applied after Type I for enhanced anti-icing protection. Each type serves specific operational needs in varying weather conditions.</p></p>
