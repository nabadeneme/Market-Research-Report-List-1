<p><p>The Green Renewable Methanol market includes two primary types: Bio Methanol and E-Methanol. Bio Methanol is produced from organic feedstocks, such as biomass or waste materials, through processes like gasification or fermentation, contributing to a circular economy. In contrast, E-Methanol is generated from renewable electricity using water and carbon dioxide, typically through electrolysis and subsequent chemical conversion. Both types serve as sustainable alternatives to fossil fuels, enabling reductions in carbon emissions and driving the transition to cleaner energy sources.</p></p>
