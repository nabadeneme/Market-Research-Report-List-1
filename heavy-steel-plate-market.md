<p><p>The heavy steel plate market comprises hot rolled and cold rolled steel plates, each with distinct characteristics. Hot rolled steel plates are produced by heating steel above its recrystallization temperature, resulting in enhanced workability and rougher surface finishes, commonly used in construction and manufacturing. In contrast, cold rolled steel plates are processed below this temperature, yielding tighter tolerances and smoother surfaces, ideal for applications where precision and aesthetics matter, such as automotive and appliance industries.</p></p>
