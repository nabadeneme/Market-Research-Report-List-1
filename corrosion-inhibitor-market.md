<p><p>The corrosion inhibitor market is primarily categorized into organic and inorganic types. Organic corrosion inhibitors are typically carbon-based compounds that function by forming protective films on metal surfaces, reducing oxidation and corrosion rates. Common examples include amines and phosphates. In contrast, inorganic corrosion inhibitors consist of metal salts or oxides that offer protection through electrochemical mechanisms. They are often used in industrial applications due to their effectiveness in high-temperature and aggressive environments, enhancing the longevity of materials and reducing maintenance costs.</p></p>
