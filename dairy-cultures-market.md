<p><ul><li>Thermophilic Type</li><li>Mesophilic Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Dairy cultures are categorized into thermophilic and mesophilic types based on their optimal fermentation temperatures. The thermophilic type thrives at higher temperatures (above 45°C), making it suitable for cheese production, particularly in harder cheeses. In contrast, mesophilic cultures operate at moderate temperatures (20-30°C), ideal for yogurt and softer cheeses. These market segments cater to distinct dairy processing needs, influencing product characteristics and consumer preferences. Both types play a vital role in the dairy industry, driving innovation and quality.</p></p>
