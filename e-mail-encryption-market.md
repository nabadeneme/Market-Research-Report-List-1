<p><p>The email encryption market consists of various types: SMTP STARTTLS, S/MIME, and PGP. SMTP STARTTLS enhances security by enabling encryption for emails sent over the Internet, protecting data in transit. S/MIME (Secure/Multipurpose Internet Mail Extensions) employs digital certificates for end-to-end email encryption, ensuring message confidentiality and authenticity. PGP (Pretty Good Privacy) uses a decentralized model with public and private keys, allowing users to encrypt and sign emails. Each type serves distinct security needs within the email communication landscape.</p></p>
