<p><p>The rail air-conditioning system market is primarily categorized into two types: air cycle air conditioning systems and vapor cycle air conditioning systems. Air cycle systems utilize air as the refrigerant, relying on a compressed air process to provide cooling, making them efficient for certain conditions. In contrast, vapor cycle systems operate using refrigerants that undergo phase changes, offering effective cooling with temperature control. Both systems enhance passenger comfort in trains, with varying applications based on performance requirements and operational efficiency.</p></p>
